Octavia E. Butler

Science Fiction Writer, Dreamer, and Creator

Octavia E. Butler is a Black, queer, science fiction writer. Throughout her life, she published over 15 books and won over 12 awards. Since her death, she continues to have awards granted to her in her honor. Her writing has played a major role in Black liberation and continues to be studied in activist spaces.

A Brief History of Octavia E. Butler's Life:

  • June 22, 1947 - Octavia was born in Pasadena, CA. Where she was primarily raised by her mother and grandmother.
  • 1959 - At age 12, Octavia started writing her first stories.
  • 1965 - After graduating high school, Octavia attended night school at a community college. She won her first award for writing in a college-wide short story contest during her freshman year. Octavia attended college during the Black Power Movement and was inspired by her, and her classmates', involvement.
  • 1971 - Octavia published her first story, "Crossover."
  • 1974 - Octavia began working on The Patternist series.
  • 1979 - Octavia began working on Kindred, which would later become her best selling novel.
  • 1984 - Octavia finished her last book of The Patternmaster series, Clay's Ark.
  • 1984 - 1989 - Octavia worked on and published her Xenogenesis trilogy.
  • 1993 - 1998 - Octavia wrote and published her The Parabole series.
  • 1995 - Olivia became the first science-fiction writer to receive the MacArthur Fellowship.
  • 1999 - After her mother's death, Octavia moves to Lake Forest Park, Washington.
  • 2003 - 2005 - Afer several years of writer's block, Octavia published multiple stories, including Fledgling.
  • February 24, 2006 - Octavia died at age 58.
